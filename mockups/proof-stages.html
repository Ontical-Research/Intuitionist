Proof Complete

Irrationality.lean Proof complete — no errors
1 theorem sqrt2_irrational :
2 Irrational (Real.sqrt 2) := by
3 by_contra h
4 rw [not_irrational_iff] at h
5 obtain ⟨a, b, hb, hab⟩ := h
6 have hcop := Int.Coprime.of_reduced a b
7 have hsq : a ^ 2 = 2 * b ^ 2 := by
8 field_simp at hab
9 linarith [sq_nonneg a, sq_nonneg b]
10 have ha : 2 ∣ a := by
11 exact Int.Prime.dvd_of_dvd_sq ⟨hsq ▸ dvd_mul_right _ _⟩
12 obtain ⟨k, hk⟩ := ha
13 have hb2 : 2 ∣ b := by
14 rw [hk] at hsq; ring_nf at hsq; omega
15 exact absurd hcop (not_coprime_of_dvd ha hb2)
Theorem. Irrationality of \(\sqrt{2}\)
\(\sqrt{2}\) is irrational.
Proof.
Suppose for contradiction that \(\sqrt{2}\) is rational.
Then \(\sqrt{2} = a/b\) for some \(a, b \in \mathbb{Z}\) with \(b \neq 0\) and \(\gcd(a,b) = 1\).
1 Squaring both sides, \(a^2 = 2b^2\). Since \(2 \mid a^2\) and \(2\) is prime, we have \(2 \mid a\).
2 Write \(a = 2k\). Substituting, \(4k^2 = 2b^2\), hence \(2 \mid b\).
But then \(2 \mid \gcd(a, b)\), contradicting \(\gcd(a, b) = 1\). \(\bot\)
∎
Design note: Completion is signaled by three things: the status bar turns green, the dashed contradiction border becomes solid, and ∎ appears. The hypothesis shelf vanishes from the paper pane — the proof is done, so there's no "current state" to show. The transition from Stage 3 → Stage 5 should feel like a door closing. The dashed → solid border transition is the visual grammar for "this block is complete." This generalizes: case blocks, sub-lemmas, anything with scope.
