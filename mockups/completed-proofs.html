Theorem. Irrationality of \(\sqrt{2}\)
\(\sqrt{2}\) is irrational; that is, there exist no integers \(a, b\) with \(b \neq 0\) such that \(\sqrt{2} = a/b\).
Proof.
Suppose for contradiction that \(\sqrt{2}\) is rational.
Then we may write \(\sqrt{2} = a/b\) where \(a, b \in \mathbb{Z}\), \(\,b \neq 0\), and \(\gcd(a, b) = 1\).
1 Squaring both sides, \(a^2 = 2b^2\). Since \(2 \mid a^2\) and \(2\) is prime, we have \(2 \mid a\).
2 Write \(a = 2k\). Substituting, \(4k^2 = 2b^2\), so \(b^2 = 2k^2\). By the same argument, \(2 \mid b\).
But then \(2 \mid \gcd(a, b)\), contradicting \(\gcd(a, b) = 1\). ↯
∎

Theorem. Infinitude of Primes
For every natural number \(n\), there exists a prime \(p \geq n\).
Proof.

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) be given. Set \(N = n! + 1\). Since \(N \geq 2\), there exists a prime \(p\) dividing \(N\).

We claim \(p \geq n\). We verify the two required conditions:

Case 1 — \(p \geq n\)
Suppose for contradiction that \(p < n\).
Then \(p \leq n\), so \(p \mid n!\) (since \(n!\) contains all factors up to \(n\)). But \(p \mid N = n! + 1\), so \(p \mid (N - n!) = 1\). This is impossible since \(p \geq 2\). ↯
Case 2 — \(p\) is prime
This holds by construction: \(p\) was chosen as a prime factor of \(N\). ✓

Therefore \(p\) is a prime with \(p \geq n\), as required.

∎

Design Notes — Things to Discuss

Contradiction blocks use a red left-border and tinted background. The ↯ marker (U+21AF, downwards zigzag arrow) signals contradiction without words. Is this the right glyph, or do we prefer something more traditional like ⊥ or ※ ?

Case blocks use a warm brown left-border to distinguish them from contradictions. Nesting a contradiction inside a case (as in Proof 2, Case 1) works visually but might get crowded with deeper nesting. How deep do we expect real proofs to go?

The tactic ↔ paper correspondence is shown by cursor position: line 4 is highlighted in Proof 1 (the by_contra), and line 13 is highlighted in Proof 2 (the case left). The paper pane should highlight the corresponding rendered block. This mockup doesn't animate that, but the real UI would.

Step markers (①②) appear in Proof 1 to mark logical steps within the contradiction. Are these helpful, or do they add clutter? They don't correspond to individual tactics — they're editorial groupings.

The hypothesis shelf (not shown in these proofs) would display current hypotheses as a compact bar: h : ¬Irrational √2 a b : ℤ etc. Shown inline below the current proof step, or pinned at the top of the paper pane?

Missing from this mockup: the chat pane (AI tutor), the symbol input dropdown, the goal display during mid-proof editing, and the library explorer. Those are separate mockups.
